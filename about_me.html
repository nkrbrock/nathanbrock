About Me

Intro

I was always interested in other languages and cultures as a child. When I was growing up, I became fascinated with Native American cultures. Seeing how different people live has always been interesting to me.

I attended the University of Northwester - St. Paul for my undergraduate where I triple majored in linguistics, intercultural studies, and biblical studies. I also double minored in Chinese studies and science & theology. It was during these years that I fell in love with minority and underrepresented languages. It was also during these years that I learned that technology can be used in amazing ways to provide practicle solutions to real problems.

I originally intended on being a Bible translator. However, after learning more about myself, I realized that this was not the path for me. I still wanted to use linguistics in a useful way, and the world of academia held to interest for me. That's when I decided to commit my professional life to the field of computational linguistics.

Training & Grad School

Because of COVID-19, I was unable to find work for over a year. I was in the final stages of a long interview process for an entry coding position when the pandemic hit and everything closed down.

I still wanted to improve my skills in programming, because I knew learning how to code could open up many new opportunities for me. Unfortunately, I wasn't able to get into grad school the first time I applied. This left me a lot of free time for an entire academic year without a job.

Even though I was very discouraged, I didn't want to give up on this path. Instead of just sitting around, I decided to take online courses that didn't require a lengthy application process. I completed Dr. Angela Yu's "100 Days of Code: The Complete Python Pro Bootcamp" as well as her "The Complete 2020 Web Development Bootcamp". I also completed courses on EdX including "Probability from the Ground Up" and "Algorithms and Data Structures" as well as earning "IBM Python Data Science" professional certificate.

In the summer of 2021, I was finally accepted into the MS in Human Language Technology through the University of Arizona. There, I've focused on taking courses that would better prepare me for a career in TTS/ASR technology.

Professional Experience

My first internship was a volunteer position with Dallas International University. Here, I worked with Chamikuro, an endangered language spoken in South America. Through this internship I learned a lot about data quality assurance as well as learning how to transcribe controlled language data using the Montreal Forced Aligner.

My second internship was through the Summer Institute of Linguistics. I worked on the AI/NLP team where I was responsible for developing and automating a workflow that would produce a text-to-speech system based off of an audio Bible and its transcript.
